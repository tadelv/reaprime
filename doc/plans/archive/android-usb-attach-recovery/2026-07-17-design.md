# Android USB attach recovery

## Problem

Android delivered `ACTION_USB_DEVICE_ATTACHED`, but `SerialServiceAndroid`
ignored it. A machine that re-enumerated remained disconnected until machine
recovery's exponential-backoff timer fired. The original observation was a
20.3-second delay; the configured backoff can reach 60 seconds.

## Design

`SerialServiceAndroid` implements the optional `DeviceAttachNotifier`
capability. It emits every attach as a non-replaying edge, including events with
null or incomplete metadata. The notifier does not filter VID, PID, product
name, or stable ID: Android may deliver the broadcast before all descriptors
are available, and the existing scan/detection path is the authority for device
support. Detach behavior is unchanged and detach or unknown actions emit no
attach hint.

`DeviceController` aggregates notifier streams and itself implements
`DeviceAttachNotifier`. Attach is deliberately not a required member of
`DeviceScanner`; BLE, Wi-Fi, simulated, and other scanner implementations do not
need placeholder streams. `ConnectionManager` checks for the optional
capability when wiring its collaborators.

`AttachReconnectCoordinator` owns the attach subscription, settle timer, burst
coalescing, pending and in-flight state, and cancellation. Android can announce
attach before the CDC interface is usable, so the coordinator waits a
configurable 500 ms before attempting recovery. Events received while settling
or while an attach-triggered attempt is in flight are absorbed rather than
queued.

The coordinator only attempts when no machine is connected and a preferred
machine exists. This prevents an unrelated USB attach from auto-connecting an
unpreferred machine or opening a picker. Its injected attempt callback invokes
normal `ConnectionManager.connect()`, preserving remembered-device
quick-connect, identity and preferred-device policy, scan fallback, ambiguity
handling when a preference exists but another machine is found, and existing
connect concurrency rules. It does not bypass detection or connect the device
named by the attach event.

Before the immediate attempt, `ConnectionManager` cancels the pending machine
recovery timer and resets its failure count. If quick-connect and scan do not
produce a machine, the coordinator invokes an explicit fallback callback that
re-arms normal machine recovery. A concurrent ordinary connect remains the
owner of its operation; an attach attempt can be declined by the existing
connect guard, after which recovery is still armed. Existing queued scale-only
work drains normally.

## Lifecycle ownership

- `SerialServiceAndroid` stores and cancels the platform USB subscription and
  closes its attach and device subjects in `dispose()`.
- `DeviceController` owns and cancels subscriptions used to aggregate notifier
  streams. Disposal during initialization prevents late subscriptions.
- `AttachReconnectCoordinator` owns and cancels its attach subscription and
  settle timer.
- `ConnectionManager` constructs and disposes the coordinator.
- Discovery-service disposal remains owned by the application/service owner;
  `DeviceController.dispose()` cancels aggregation but does not dispose the
  services it was given.

## Scope

No REST, WebSocket, database, BLE transport, Wi-Fi, simulated-device, or scale
policy changes are introduced. Improved real-hardware timing is not claimed
until this rebased implementation is measured on Android hardware.
